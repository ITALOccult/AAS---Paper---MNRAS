\subsection{Extended Phase Space and the Sundman Transformation}
\label{sec:extended_phase_space}

We begin with the separable heliocentric Hamiltonian
\begin{equation}
  \begin{aligned}[t]
    H(\vq, \vp) &= T(\vp) + V(\vq), \\
    T &= \frac{|\vp|^2}{2}, \\
    V &= -\frac{\mu}{|\vq|} + V_{J_2}(\vq),
  \end{aligned}
  \label{eq:H_separable}
\end{equation}
where $\vq \in \mathbb{R}^3$ is the heliocentric position in \pAU,
$\vp$ is the conjugate momentum in \pAUd, and
$\mu = \mathrm{GMS} = 2.9591\times10^{-4}\,\mathrm{AU}^3\,\mathrm{day}^{-2}$
\citep{constants_iau2015}.
The Sundman transformation $dt = g(\vq)\,ds$ replaces physical
time $t$ with a pseudo-time $s$ in which the integration step
$\Delta s$ is fixed while the physical step
$\Delta t = g(\vq)\,\Delta s$ varies with position.

To embed this transformation within a Hamiltonian framework we
introduce the \textit{extended phase space}
$\mathcal{E} = T^*(\mathbb{R}^3 \times \mathbb{R})$, with
coordinates $(\vq, t, \vp, p_t)$, where $t$ is promoted to a
coordinate and $p_t = -H(\vq,\vp)$ is its conjugate momentum.
The extended Hamiltonian is
\begin{equation}
  \Gamma(\vq, \vp, t, p_t)
    = g(\vq)\bigl[H(\vq, \vp) + p_t\bigr]
    = g(\vq)\bigl[T(\vp) + V(\vq) + p_t\bigr].
  \label{eq:extended_hamiltonian}
\end{equation}
Hamilton's equations with respect to $s$ reproduce the original
equations of motion together with $dt/ds = g(\vq)$
\citep{PretoTremaine1999,HairerLubichWanner2006}.

\subsubsection{Separability of the Extended Hamiltonian}
\label{sec:separability}

A symplectic splitting method requires $\Gamma$ to be expressible
as $\Gamma = \tilde{T} + \tilde{V}$ where $\tilde{T}$ depends
only on $\vp$ or $\tilde{V}$ depends only on $\vq$.
Substituting \eqref{eq:H_separable} into
\eqref{eq:extended_hamiltonian}:
\begin{equation}
  \Gamma = \underbrace{g(\vq)\,T(\vp)}_{\tilde{T}(\vq,\vp)}
         + \underbrace{g(\vq)\bigl[V(\vq) + p_t\bigr]}_{\tilde{V}(\vq,p_t)}.
  \label{eq:Gamma_split}
\end{equation}
The term $\tilde{T} = g(\vq)|\vp|^2/2$ depends on both $\vq$
and $\vp$, so the splitting is \textit{not} separable in the
standard sense unless $g$ is constant.
However, a weaker condition suffices for symplecticity: it is
enough that the maps generated by $\tilde{T}$ and $\tilde{V}$
separately are each \textit{exactly integrable}, i.e., admit
closed-form solutions \citep{BlanesIserles2012}.

\paragraph{Map generated by $\tilde{V}$.}
Since $\tilde{V}$ depends only on $(\vq, p_t)$, the
$\tilde{V}$-flow advances $\vp$ and $t$ at fixed $(\vq, p_t)$:
\begin{equation}
  \begin{aligned}[t]
    \vp &\leftarrow \vp - h\,\nabla_\vq \tilde{V} \\
        &= \vp - h\,\bigl[(\nabla_\vq g)(V + p_t)
                         + g\,\nabla_\vq V\bigr], \\
    t &\leftarrow t + h\,g(\vq).
  \end{aligned}
  \label{eq:V_flow}
\end{equation}
This is a shear map in $(\vq,\vp)$ space — exactly the Kick
operator $\mathcal{K}(h)$ with modified force
$\tilde{\mathbf{f}} = -\nabla_\vq\tilde{V}$.

\paragraph{Map generated by $\tilde{T}$.}
The $\tilde{T}$-flow at fixed $p_t$ satisfies
\begin{equation}
  \dot{\vq} = \nabla_\vp \tilde{T} = g(\vq)\,\vp,
  \qquad
  \dot{\vp} = -\nabla_\vq \tilde{T}
            = -\tfrac{1}{2}(\nabla_\vq g)|\vp|^2.
  \label{eq:T_flow_ode}
\end{equation}
For the Keplerian scaling $g \propto r^{3/2}$ this system is
exactly integrable via the Kepler solver \citep{BlanesIserles2012}.
For the discrete leapfrog used here, we approximate the
$\tilde{T}$-flow by a single Drift step
$\vq \leftarrow \vq + h\,g(\vq)\,\vp$, discarding the
$\dot{\vp}$ correction — this introduces a splitting error that
is accounted for in the order analysis of
Section~\ref{sec:shadow}.
